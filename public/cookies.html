Introduction

This Cookie Policy explains how Paperlyte ("we", "us", or "our") uses cookies and similar technologies when you visit our website (the "Service"). It describes what these technologies are, why we use them, and the choices you have to control their use. This policy should be read together with our Privacy Policy.

Paperlyte is built privacy-first. We keep our use of cookies to the minimum needed to operate the Service, and we do not use cookies to build advertising profiles or sell your data.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work, to remember your preferences, and to provide information to site operators. "Similar technologies" include browser localStorage, sessionStorage, and other client-side storage that functions like a cookie. In this policy, references to "cookies" cover all of these technologies.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

2.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies

These are essential for the Service to function and cannot be switched off in our systems. They are used to remember basic state such as your cookie consent choice and core interface preferences. Where possible, this information is stored on your own device rather than transmitted to us.

2.2 Functional Cookies

These remember choices you make to give you a better experience, such as your theme (light/dark) preference and your cookie consent selection. Disabling these may reduce the convenience of the Service but will not prevent core writing and organization from working.

2.3 Analytics Cookies

Where we measure how our site is used, we use privacy-focused, cookie-less analytics to understand aggregate, anonymized traffic so we can improve the site. This does not set cookies, does not collect personal data, and does not track you across sites or devices. We do not use third-party advertising or marketing cookies.

2.4 Cookies We Do Not Use

  • Advertising or retargeting cookies
  • Cross-site tracking or social media tracking pixels
  • Cookies that sell or share your data with data brokers

4. Legal Compliance

We handle cookies in line with applicable laws, including the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and ePrivacy rules, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA), and other comparable regulations. Where the law requires consent for non-essential cookies, we will ask for it before such cookies are set, and strictly necessary cookies are used on the basis of our legitimate interest in operating the Service.

5. Your Controls and Choices

You are in control of how cookies are used. You can:

  • Control or delete cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers let you block or remove cookies and clear local storage at any time.
  • Use browser "Do Not Track" or Global Privacy Control signals; because our analytics are cookie-less and we do not sell data, your privacy is preserved by default.

Blocking strictly necessary cookies may cause parts of the Service to stop working as intended.

6. Data Retention

Strictly necessary and functional cookies persist only as long as needed for their purpose — session cookies are cleared when you close your browser, while persistent preference cookies last up to 12 months unless you delete them sooner. Our analytics provider does not set cookies and stores only anonymized, aggregate metrics.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in technology, law, or our practices. When we do, we will revise the "Last Updated" date above. Material changes will be communicated through the Service where appropriate.
