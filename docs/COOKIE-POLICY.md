# Cookie Policy

**Last Updated:** June 27, 2026

## Introduction

This Cookie Policy explains how Paperlyte ("we", "us", "our") uses cookies and
similar technologies (such as `localStorage` and `sessionStorage`) when you
visit our website or use our note-taking application. It should be read together
with our [Privacy Policy](./PRIVACY-POLICY.md), which describes how we handle
your personal data.

Paperlyte is **privacy-first and local-first by design**. We use the minimum set
of storage technologies needed to operate the service, and we do **not** use
third-party advertising cookies or cross-site tracking pixels.

---

## 1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website.
They are widely used to make websites work, to remember your preferences, and to
provide information to site owners. "Similar technologies" include browser
storage mechanisms such as `localStorage` and `sessionStorage`, which Paperlyte
uses for local-first functionality (for example, keeping your notes and settings
available offline).

---

## 2. Types of Cookies We Use

| Type        | Purpose                                   | Examples                                   | Duration         |
| ----------- | ----------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ | ---------------- |
| Essential   | Authentication, security, core app state  | Session token, CSRF protection             | Session / 1 year |
| Functional  | Remember your preferences                 | Theme (light/dark), reduced-motion setting | Up to 1 year     |
| Analytics   | Understand aggregate, anonymized usage    | Privacy-friendly page/usage metrics        | Up to 1 year     |

**Essential cookies** are required for the service to function and cannot be
switched off in our systems. They are usually set only in response to actions
you take, such as signing in or setting your privacy preferences.

**Functional cookies and local storage** remember choices you make (such as your
theme) to give you a better, more personalized experience.

**Analytics cookies** help us understand how the application is used so we can
improve it. Any analytics data is aggregated and anonymized, and we do not use
it to identify individual users.

We do **not** use marketing or advertising cookies.

---

## 3. Third-Party Cookies

We do **not** use third-party advertising cookies or tracking pixels. Where a
trusted service provider (for example, our hosting or error-monitoring provider)
sets a cookie strictly to deliver the service you requested, it is limited to
that purpose and governed by a data processing agreement. See the "Data Sharing
& Third Parties" section of our [Privacy Policy](./PRIVACY-POLICY.md) for more
detail on our service providers.

---

## 4. Legal Compliance

We handle cookies in accordance with applicable laws and regulations, including:

- **GDPR / UK GDPR:** For users in the EU/EEA and UK, non-essential cookies
  (functional and analytics) are set only with your consent, which you may
  withdraw at any time. Essential cookies are used on the basis of legitimate
  interest in providing a working, secure service.
- **CCPA / CPRA:** California residents have the right to know what information is
  collected and to opt out of any "sale" of personal information. We do not sell
  personal information.
- **ePrivacy Directive ("Cookie Law"):** Where required, we present a consent
  mechanism before setting non-essential cookies.

---

## 5. Your Choices and Controls

You can control and manage cookies in several ways:

- **In-app settings:** Disable analytics in the application's privacy settings.
- **Consent banner:** Where shown, accept or reject non-essential cookies, and
  change your choice at any time.
- **Browser settings:** Block or delete cookies and clear local storage through
  your browser settings. Note that blocking essential cookies may prevent parts
  of the service from working.
- **Do Not Track (DNT):** We respect DNT browser signals.

---

## 6. Data Retention

Cookie and local-storage lifetimes are summarized in the table in Section 2.
Session cookies are deleted when you close your browser. Persistent cookies and
stored preferences remain until they expire or until you delete them. For how
long we retain the personal data associated with your account, see the "Data
Retention" section of our [Privacy Policy](./PRIVACY-POLICY.md).
